JunKyu Lee's Research

Research Statement

I study how efficiency-oriented design choices induce structured instabilities in neural systems, and how such instabilities can be characterized and controlled.

Many efficient AI techniques—e.g., quantization, pruning, sparsity, low-rank adaptation, and systems-level constraints—are often evaluated primarily through average accuracy or throughput. My work treats efficiency as a first-class system transformation that can reshape system dynamics, producing structured failure modes (instability, brittleness, and unpredictability) that are not captured by standard metrics. I develop methods to (i) make these instabilities explicit, (ii) characterize them with principled measurements and benchmarks, and (iii) design control and correction mechanisms that enable predictable and reliable efficient AI.

Robotics (e.g., legged locomotion) is used as a concrete testbed where efficiency–instability–control trade-offs are unavoidable and measurable.
